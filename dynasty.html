01 · A generational Greek shipping house

One family, three tickers, fifty years at sea.

The bid for Genco is the newest chapter in a much older story: a Chios-rooted shipping family that has run public companies in New York for two decades and treated the listed vehicle as its native habitat.

The patriarch

Simeon Palios

The Diana group traces to Simeon P. Palios, a Chios-born naval architect and marine engineer who has worked in shipping since 1969 and founded Diana Shipping Agencies S.A. in 1972.1 Trained at Durham University and once an ensign in the Greek Navy inspecting passenger vessels, he built a group that at its height controlled more than fifty ships across dry bulk, containers and tankers.2 Diana Shipping Inc. was incorporated in the Marshall Islands in late 2004 to consolidate the dry bulk business for a public listing, and floated on the New York Stock Exchange in March 2005, raising roughly $217 million.3

The house style has been conservative from the start: modern tonnage on fixed-rate period charters, built to deliver predictable quarterly cash flow rather than to chase the spot market. Palios stepped back as Diana's chief executive in early 2021 but remains chairman, and the Lloyd's List Greek Shipping Awards named the group's holding company Dry Cargo Company of the Year for 2023, an award he accepted in person.4

A note on the name

The public record does not document why the house is called Diana. The name attaches to the 1972 Athens agency, decades before the listed company, and neither the filings nor the founder's interviews explain its origin. We flag it as unresolved rather than guess.

The succession

Two daughters, two companies, one clean split

The 2020 handover was not gradual drift. On specific dates in February 2020, the group's leadership divided along family lines, with one daughter and the senior professional lieutenants taking the dry bulk parent, and the other daughter and her husband taking the tanker vehicle.

Diana Shipping (NYSE: DSX)

Semiramis Paliou

The operator-engineer. She holds a BSc in Mechanical Engineering from Imperial College London and an MSc in Naval Architecture from University College London, began as a trainee ship surveyor at Lloyd's Register from 1996 to 1998, and has worked in the group ever since.5 She became Diana's chief executive in 2021 and runs the bid for Genco. She also chaired OceanPal and sits across the major Greek and international maritime bodies, from the Union of Greek Shipowners to the Global Maritime Forum.5

Performance Shipping (NASDAQ: PSHG)

Aliki Paliou

The other daughter, from a different world entirely. She studied Theatre Studies at the University of Kent, took an MA in Scenography at Central Saint Martins, and graduated from the Greek School of Fine Art in Athens in 2005.6 She chairs Performance Shipping and is married to its chief executive, Andreas Michalopoulos.6 Together they pivoted the company out of container ships into Aframax tankers.

The connective tissue

Two non-family executives bind the vehicles together. Ioannis Zafirakis, now Diana's president, spent 2010 to 2020 as a director and senior executive of the company that became Performance Shipping before moving to Diana.7 Anastasios Margaronis, a Diana director and former Performance president, personally holds a stake in OceanPal. The same small circle has sat on multiple sides of these companies for years.

The structure

Three listed vehicles, three different ways to keep control

The strategically revealing fact about this family is that it owns very different amounts of each company, and has bolted on exactly as much voting machinery as each situation required.

DSX · NYSE

Diana Shipping

The dry bulk parent and the aggressor in the Genco bid. One share, one vote, no super-voting class. The family is an anchor holder in the low-to-mid 20% range; control rests on ownership and board seats, not special rights.8

PSHG · NASDAQ

Performance Shipping

The Aframax tanker arm, chaired by Aliki Paliou. Here the family holds modest common stock but roughly 90% of the votes, through Series C super-voting preferred carrying ten votes each, held via the vehicles Mango and Mitzela.9

SVRN · NASDAQ

OceanPal → SovereignAI

Spun out of Diana in 2021 with three older bulkers. Control was kept twice over: Diana's own super-voting preferred plus Semiramis Paliou's personal Series E shares. In late 2025 it announced the SovereignAI treasury strategy and changed its Nasdaq ticker to SVRN.10

OceanPal · the strangest branch

From three bulkers to a token treasury

OceanPal began in November 2021 as a classic dropdown vehicle: Diana distributed one OceanPal share for every ten Diana shares, contributed three of its oldest dry bulk carriers, and kept control through super-voting preferred entitling it to up to 34% of the vote.11 For two years it behaved like a small, spot-exposed bulk owner managed by the family's own ship managers.

Then it swerved. In August 2025 it ran a reverse stock split; in October 2025 it announced a $120 million investment alongside the NEAR Foundation to launch a subsidiary called SovereignAI; and on November 17, 2025 it changed its Nasdaq ticker from OP to SVRN.12 By mid-November the subsidiary held roughly 53.9 million NEAR tokens worth about $133 million.12 By May 2026 the company had received a Nasdaq delinquency notice.13 By June 2026 its maritime division was described as three vessels: two Panamax dry bulk carriers and one MR2 product tanker. A dry bulk spin-off had become, in effect, a digital-asset treasury wrapped around a small mixed fleet.

Where Genco fits

The dynasty reaches outside the family

Genco Shipping & Trading is not a Palios company. It is the largest U.S.-headquartered dry bulk owner, founded in 2004 by Peter Georgiopoulos, taken through Chapter 11 in 2014, and rebuilt since 2021 into a low-debt, dividend-focused fleet of 43 vessels under chief executive John Wobensmith.14 What ties it to this story is that Diana has spent 2025 and 2026 trying to absorb it, accumulating a roughly 14% stake and pressing an unsolicited approach.

That makes Genco the dynasty's most ambitious move yet: not a spin-off it controls, not a sister vehicle it can steer with preferred stock, but a larger, widely held peer it must win on price and persuasion.
