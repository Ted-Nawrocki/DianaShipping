The thesis

Corporate actions are rarely just paperwork.

Behind the tender offers and rights plans of 2026 sits a generational Greek shipping house: founded in Athens, incorporated under the flag of the Marshall Islands, listed in New York, and run by a second generation that has spent the decade engineering control across three public companies.

The family's activity traces to Diana Shipping Agencies, established in 1972 by Simeon Palios.1 From that root grew Diana Shipping, a conservative dry bulk owner now led by his daughter Semiramis Paliou; Performance Shipping, an Aframax tanker company chaired by his daughter Aliki Paliou; and OceanPal, a 2021 spin-off that in late 2025 announced a SovereignAI crypto-treasury strategy and changed its Nasdaq ticker from OP to SVRN.2

In 2026 Diana moved on Genco Shipping & Trading, the largest United States headquartered dry bulk owner. The bid climbed from an initial proposal to a revised offer valued at 27.34 dollars per share, combining cash and one Diana share.3 Genco's board refused to engage, armed with a shareholder rights plan adopted in anticipation of exactly this kind of approach. What follows is the dossier: the dynasty, the family, the deal, the defense, and the flag state that ties it all together.

As of 27 June 2026

This is a live situation. The revised offer is outstanding, the proxy vote of 18 June went against Diana, and the tender was set to expire on 26 June with the result not yet public at the time of writing. Every page is dated and sourced to primary filings.
