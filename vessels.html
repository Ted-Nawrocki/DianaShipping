Performance Shipping · NASDAQ: PSHG

As of 2 March 2026 Performance reported a fleet of fourteen tankers, including Aframax and Suezmax tonnage on the water, with newbuild LR2 Aframax and Suezmax vessels scheduled through 2029.3 The renewal program has been replacing older Aframaxes with LNG-ready, scrubber-fitted LR2 vessels of roughly 114,000 dwt; the first, P. Massport, was delivered in 2025 on a five-year charter to a Gunvor subsidiary.4 The complete current roster is maintained on the company's fleet page.

OceanPal / SovereignAI · NASDAQ: SVRN

OceanPal remains the smallest of the family vehicles, with a fleet reported at three dry bulk vessels, a Capesize and two Panamaxes, managed by the group's own ship managers.5 Its 2025 reinvention into the SovereignAI digital-asset treasury, and the ticker change from OP to SVRN, has made the crypto strategy, not the ships, the company's defining feature. The current fleet is detailed in its SEC filings. The full arc is on the dynasty page.
