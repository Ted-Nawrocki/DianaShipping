Genco Shipping & Trading · NYSE: GNK

43 vessels, the largest US-headquartered bulk fleet

As of April 2026 Genco reported 43 dry bulk carriers, average age about 12.5 years, aggregate capacity roughly 4,934,000 dwt, split between major bulk (Newcastlemax and Capesize) and minor bulk (Ultramax and Supramax).2 The fleet is in constant renewal: in early 2026 Genco sold the 2005-built Supramaxes Genco Picardy and Genco Predator and agreed to buy a 2019 Imabari-built 182,000 dwt Capesize for $65 million, delivering June 2026.2

Published Capesize names include Genco Augustus, Bear, Constantine, Courageous, Defender, Endeavour, Intrepid, Liberty, Lion, London, Ranger, Reliance, Resolute and Tiberius; minor-bulk names include Genco Constellation, Enterprise, Magic, Freedom, Columbia, Hunter, Rhone, Pyrenees, Languedoc, Brittany, Bourgogne, Auvergne, Wasp, Scorpion, Mantis and Hornet.3 Because the gencoshipping.com hull pages can trail recent transactions, the authoritative complete list is the fleet table in Genco's current Form 10-K.

Performance Shipping · NASDAQ: PSHG

A modern tanker fleet, fourteen hulls

As of March 2, 2026 Performance reported a fleet of fourteen tankers, including Aframax and Suezmax tonnage on the water, with newbuild LR2 Aframax and Suezmax vessels scheduled through 2029.4 The renewal program has been replacing older Aframaxes with LNG-ready, scrubber-fitted LR2 vessels of roughly 114,000 dwt; the first, P. Massport, was delivered in 2025 on a five-year charter to a Gunvor subsidiary.5 The complete current roster is maintained on the company's fleet page.

OceanPal / SovereignAI · NASDAQ: SVRN

Three bulkers, and a pivot away from the sea

OceanPal remains the smallest of the family vehicles, with a fleet reported at three dry bulk vessels, a Capesize and two Panamaxes, managed by the group's own ship managers.6 Its 2025 reinvention into the SovereignAI digital-asset treasury, and the ticker change from OP to SVRN, has made the crypto strategy, not the ships, the company's defining feature. The current fleet is detailed in its SEC filings. The full arc is on the dynasty page.
